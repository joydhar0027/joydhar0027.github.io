Multimodal Reliable Clinical AI

Profile

Building trustworthy AI where clinical reality is noisy, multimodal, and high-stakes.

I work on heterogeneous medical data that spans imaging, multi-omics, electronic health records, and temporal signals. My primary goal is to build reliable models that remain robust under distribution shift, adversarial noise, and data scarcity while staying computationally viable for real-world healthcare systems.

Current Focus

  • Multimodal representation learning for medical analysis
  • Adversarial and empirical robustness benchmarking
  • Efficient architectures for resource-constrained deployment
  • Self-supervised and generative design for healthcare AI

Tooling

Languages: Python, C/C++, Java, C#, PHP, MATLAB

ML Stack: TensorFlow, Keras, PyTorch, scikit-learn

Data Stack: NumPy, Pandas, OpenCV, SciPy

Workflow: Jupyter, Colab, LaTeX, VS Code, Kaggle

Research Mission

From lab-grade performance to clinically dependable AI.

The work emphasizes model behavior under uncertainty and practical transferability, so systems can move from benchmark success to resilient clinical utility.
